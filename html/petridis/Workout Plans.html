Workout Plans

Beginner Plan

  • Day 1: Full Body Workout (Light Weights) - Focus on form and technique with basic exercises like squats, push-ups, and dumbbell rows.
  • Day 2: Rest or Stretch - Incorporate gentle yoga or dynamic stretches to aid recovery.
  • Day 3: Upper Body Focus - Target shoulders, chest, and back with exercises like shoulder presses, chest presses, and pull-ups.
  • Day 4: Rest or Light Cardio - Go for a 20-minute brisk walk or cycling session.
  • Day 5: Lower Body Focus - Include lunges, deadlifts, and leg presses for strength building.
  • Day 6: Rest - Prioritize hydration and adequate sleep for muscle recovery.
  • Day 7: Full Body Stretch - Stretch all major muscle groups for flexibility and relaxation.

Intermediate Plan

  • Day 1: Push Day (Chest, Shoulders, Triceps) - Include bench presses, overhead presses, and tricep dips for strength and endurance.
  • Day 2: Pull Day (Back, Biceps) - Focus on pull-ups, barbell rows, and bicep curls to build muscle definition.
  • Day 3: Rest or Cardio - Perform a 30-minute moderate cardio session like jogging or rowing.
  • Day 4: Leg Day (Quads, Hamstrings, Calves) - Perform squats, Romanian deadlifts, and calf raises for lower body power.
  • Day 5: Rest - Engage in active recovery with light stretching or a slow-paced walk.
  • Day 6: Full Body Compound Lifts - Incorporate exercises like deadlifts, cleans, and thrusters for overall strength.
  • Day 7: Active Recovery - Do a low-impact activity such as swimming or yoga to restore energy.
